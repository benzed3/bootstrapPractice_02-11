Godzilla (1954)

What can be said about the original 1954 Godzilla? The film is simply a masterpiece. Ishiro Honda’s film begun what is now the longest running film franchise, also with the most films, with 37 to date with one slated for next year, 2024. The acting and special effects are corny and not up to our current standards, but it still happens to be a great film. The first time I saw this, I was impressed with how well they did with the special effects, considering the limitations back then. Godzilla still appeared menacing. The destruction is apparent. The practical effects remained for many years, but they have worked. Well, at least for this film it works really well. The acting may be a little dramatic by our standards, but it still drives the point. Not until the recent Godzilla Minus One (Gojira Mainasu Wan in Japan) has a movie about a giant monster been engaging. Sure, the other Godzilla films are fun to watch, but that is all they have been: just some monster fighting action. Godzilla has as its theme the dangers of nuclear weapons, with Godzilla being symbolic of the potential destruction of nuclear arms. Created from nuclear bombs dropped by the United States in Japan, Godzilla is a mutated prehistoric animal. On the fictional Odo island, local legend tells of how innocent girls were given and fed to ward of Godzilla. So only on this island is Godzilla known. That is, until he attacks random fishing boats off Japanese waters. Strange disasters also occur on Odo Island, caused by Godzilla. This leads research scientists to Odo island to investigate the source of these attacks, and there those scientists encounter Godzilla. The shot, with Godzilla’s head seen sticking up behind the hills, is classic. This is the first view in the film of Godzilla. The researchers, in fear, follow Godzilla, but find that he has returned to the ocean’s waters. At a conference, lead scientist, archaeologist Dr. Kyohei Yamane, explains Godzilla’s origins. The conference ends in a fight of whether to tell the public or not. Godzilla attacks around Tokyo Bay, but he is warded off by planes. Preparations for the next attack are made. This next time, Godzilla attacks full force, causing utter destruction. All the preparations made to stop him fail. After the destruction, the devastation caused by Godzilla’s attack are clearly seen, as is the way people are coping with it. Emiko, Yamane’s daughter, breaks Serisawa’s trust, but for good reason. She, along with her boyfriend, plead with Serisawa. At first he says no, but seeing the destruction caused by Godzilla, he decides to use the oxygen destroyer, but burns and gets rid of any plans to prevent another one from being built. Off at sea, Godzilla is found using a Geiger counter. Serisawa convines Ogata to let him go down with him into the ocean, since he is the only one to use the oxygen destroyer. Ogata reluctantly agrees. Once down, Serisawa sends Ogata back to the surface, sets off the oxygen destroyer, which kills Godzilla and himself. Those on the boat mourn Serisawa and his sacrifice. Godzilla is dead, but at great cost.
